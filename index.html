What is a tracking parameter?

Every time you share a link from Facebook, a newsletter, or a Google ad, the platform silently appends surveillance tags to the URL. Tags like utm_source=newsletter, fbclid=IwAR2..., or ?s=46 tell the destination site exactly where you came from and who you are.

They do nothing for the person receiving the link — they just bulk it up and expose browsing behavior. PastePure strips them before the link leaves your clipboard.

Frequently asked questions

Does PastePure send my URLs anywhere?

No. The web tool runs entirely in your browser — JavaScript parses and cleans the URL locally. Nothing is transmitted to a server. The Mac app works the same way: it reads and writes your clipboard on-device only.

Will removing tracking parameters break the link?

No. Tracking parameters are read by analytics tools after the page loads — they have no effect on routing. The destination page is identical with or without them. PastePure only removes parameters it is certain are tracking-only.

What is UTM, and why should I care?

UTM stands for Urchin Tracking Module — a standard from Google Analytics. Marketers append utm_source, utm_medium, and similar tags to links so they can measure campaign performance. They're harmless but noisy, and they expose which email or ad drove your click to the destination site.

Does the Mac app work without an internet connection?

Yes. PastePure for Mac is entirely offline. It reads your clipboard, applies cleaning rules locally, and writes the result back — no network request is made at any point.

Which macOS version is required for the Mac app?

PastePure requires macOS 14 Sonoma or later.

Does PastePure affect links where the parameters matter?

PastePure is conservative. For YouTube it keeps v (video ID), t (timestamp), and list (playlist). For Amazon it preserves the product ASIN. It only removes parameters that are universally recognized as tracking-only.
